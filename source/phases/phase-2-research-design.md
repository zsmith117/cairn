# Phase 2: Research Design

Planning your study methodology, participant criteria, and research materials.

---

## Phase Overview

**Objective:** Design a rigorous research study with clear methodology, defined participant criteria, and complete session materials.

**Duration:** Typically 1–3 weeks depending on study complexity.

**Key Outcomes:**

- Research plan documented with methodology rationale
- Participant screening criteria defined
- Interview/moderation guide developed
- All session materials prepared

---

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Methodology Selection

Document your research approach:

- Select appropriate research method(s)
- Define sample size and rationale
- Identify any methodological constraints
- Document assumptions and limitations

> Reference [UXR-004: Research Planning & Scoping](#sop/UXR-004/procedure) for methodology selection guidance.

### 2. Participant Definition

Define who you need to talk to:

- Document participant criteria (inclusion/exclusion)
- Determine screening questions
- Calculate recruitment targets
- Identify recruitment channels

> Reference [UXR-003: Participant Recruitment & Screening](#sop/UXR-003/procedure) for detailed recruitment procedures.

### 3. Materials Development

Create all research materials:

- Develop interview/moderation guide
- Create any stimuli or prototypes needed
- Prepare screening questionnaire
- Draft participant communications

> Reference [UXR-005: Interview/Moderation Guide Development](#sop/UXR-005/procedure) for guide development best practices.

### 4. Pilot & Refine

Test your materials:

- Conduct pilot session if possible
- Review timing and flow
- Refine questions based on pilot
- Finalize all materials

## Phase Completion Checklist

Before moving to Phase 3, confirm:

- [ ] Research methodology documented with rationale
- [ ] Participant criteria defined and screening questions ready
- [ ] Interview/moderation guide complete
- [ ] All stimuli and materials prepared
- [ ] Pilot session conducted (if applicable)
- [ ] Recruitment ready to begin
